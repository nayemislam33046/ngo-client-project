ALHAJ SHAMSUL HOQUE FOUNDATION

Alhaj Shamsul Hoque Foundation (ASHF) is a registered non-governmental voluntary organization that holds Special Consultative Status with the United Nations ECOSOC. Since 2018, it has been working across various districts of Bangladesh—such as Chattogram, Cox's Bazar, Kurigram, Sunamganj, and Satkhira—standing beside the poor, vulnerable, and disaster-affected communities.

ASHF serves as a humanitarian bridge, connecting those in need with those willing to help. The organization has provided shelter, food, education, healthcare, safe water and sanitation (WASH), and emergency relief. It has also actively supported the Rohingya population (FDMNs) with compassion and commitment.

Ongoing Projects

Emergency Solar Power Project – Gaza

In the war-torn Khan Yunis area of Gaza, solar power systems have been installed in electricity-deprived communities. Amid Gaza's darkness, a light of hope is shining — a joint effort by Alhaj Shamsul Hoque Foundation and Saba Sanabil Foundation.

1,000 Tubewell Project

A major initiative has begun to install 1,000 tubewells (hand pumps) in remote and hard-to-reach areas of Bangladesh to provide safe drinking water. This project will transform lives in regions suffering from severe drought and lack of safe water, Insha'Allah.

Masjid-e-Fatema — Spreading the Light of Faith

Construction of Masjid-e-Fatema in Birganj, Dinajpur, is progressing rapidly. This mosque is inspiring the local community to return to prayer and embrace an Islamic way of life. It is not just a building but a focal point for spiritual transformation.

Severe winter has begun in Gaza. ASHF Stands by the Family of Martyr Saiful Islam

Alhaj Shamsul Hoque Foundation has provided 100,000 Taka from its own fund to the family of Martyr Advocate Saiful Islam Alif. The bereaved family includes a 2.5-year-old child, a pregnant wife, and an unborn baby. A fundraising campaign is underway to raise 10 million Taka for their future security.
